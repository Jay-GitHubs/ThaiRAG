## 1. Introduction and Purpose

### 1.1 About This Handbook

This Employee Handbook serves as a comprehensive guide to the policies, procedures, and expectations that govern employment at Acme Corporation. Every employee, regardless of position, department, or tenure, is expected to read, understand, and comply with the policies outlined in this document. This handbook supersedes all prior versions and any informal agreements that may have existed before its publication.

Acme Corporation reserves the right to modify, amend, or discontinue any policy described herein at any time, with or without prior notice. Employees will be notified of material changes through the company intranet and official email communications. It is each employee's responsibility to stay informed of current policies.

### 1.2 Company Mission and Values

Acme Corporation was founded in 2005 with a singular mission: to deliver innovative technology solutions that empower businesses to operate more efficiently and sustainably. Our core values guide every decision we make:

- **Integrity:** We conduct business honestly and transparently, holding ourselves to the highest ethical standards in every interaction with colleagues, customers, partners, and the broader community.
- **Innovation:** We foster a culture of continuous improvement, encouraging creative thinking and calculated risk-taking to develop solutions that address real-world problems.
- **Collaboration:** We believe that diverse perspectives produce the best outcomes. We work across teams, departments, and geographies to leverage collective expertise.
- **Customer Focus:** Our customers are at the center of everything we do. We strive to understand their needs deeply and deliver solutions that exceed their expectations.
- **Accountability:** We take ownership of our work, our commitments, and our impact. Every team member is empowered to make decisions and is responsible for the outcomes of those decisions.

### 1.3 Employment at Will

Employment at Acme Corporation is on an at-will basis unless otherwise specified in a written employment agreement signed by an authorized officer of the company. This means that either the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice, subject to applicable law. Nothing in this handbook creates or is intended to create a contract of employment or any binding obligation on the part of Acme Corporation.

### 1.4 Equal Opportunity Statement

Acme Corporation is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic protected by applicable federal, state, or local law. This commitment applies to all aspects of employment, including recruitment, hiring, promotion, compensation, benefits, training, transfer, and termination.

## 2. Employee Code of Conduct

### 2.1 General Expectations

All Acme Corporation employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally and ethically at all times. This applies to interactions with colleagues, customers, vendors, partners, and the general public. Employees serve as representatives of the company, and their behavior reflects on the organization as a whole.

Professional conduct includes, but is not limited to:

- Treating all individuals with dignity, respect, and courtesy
- Communicating openly, honestly, and constructively
- Meeting deadlines and fulfilling commitments reliably
- Maintaining a positive and productive work environment
- Resolving conflicts through appropriate channels
- Supporting team goals and organizational objectives
- Representing the company accurately and positively in all external interactions

### 2.2 Conflict of Interest

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict, or appear to conflict, with the interests of Acme Corporation. A conflict of interest arises when an employee's personal activities, relationships, or financial interests could impair their ability to act in the company's best interest or could create the appearance of impropriety.

Examples of potential conflicts of interest include:

- Holding a significant financial interest in a competitor, supplier, or customer of Acme Corporation
- Engaging in outside employment or consulting that competes with or interferes with the employee's duties at Acme Corporation
- Using company resources, information, or relationships for personal gain
- Hiring, supervising, or making business decisions involving family members or close personal associates without disclosure
- Accepting gifts, entertainment, or favors from vendors, suppliers, or customers that could influence business decisions

All potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed in writing to the employee's manager and the Human Resources department. The company will evaluate each situation on a case-by-case basis and determine the appropriate course of action.

### 2.3 Confidentiality

Employees have access to proprietary and confidential information in the course of their employment. This information is a valuable asset of Acme Corporation and must be protected at all times. Confidential information includes, but is not limited to:

- Trade secrets, inventions, and intellectual property
- Customer lists, contracts, and business relationships
- Financial data, projections, and business plans
- Employee records, compensation information, and performance evaluations
- Marketing strategies, pricing models, and competitive analyses
- Software source code, algorithms, and technical specifications
- Internal communications, meeting notes, and strategic discussions

Employees must not disclose confidential information to unauthorized persons, whether inside or outside the company, during or after employment. All confidential information must be stored securely and accessed only on a need-to-know basis. Upon termination of employment, all confidential materials must be returned to the company.

### 2.4 Use of Company Property

Company property, including physical assets, technology, and intellectual property, is provided for business purposes. Limited personal use of certain company property may be permitted, provided it does not interfere with work responsibilities, violate any company policy, or result in additional costs to the company.

Company property includes:

- Computers, laptops, tablets, and mobile devices
- Software licenses and cloud services
- Office equipment, supplies, and furniture
- Vehicles, tools, and specialized equipment
- Company credit cards and purchasing accounts
- Brand assets, logos, and marketing materials

Employees are responsible for the proper care and maintenance of company property in their possession. Any loss, damage, or theft of company property must be reported immediately to the employee's manager and the IT department.

### 2.5 Social Media and Public Communications

Employees are free to engage in social media and public communications on their own time and using their own devices. However, when doing so, employees must be mindful that their statements may be associated with Acme Corporation. The following guidelines apply:

- Clearly identify personal opinions as your own and not those of Acme Corporation
- Do not disclose confidential company information on any social media platform
- Do not use company logos, trademarks, or branding without authorization
- Do not engage in communications that could be perceived as harassment, discrimination, or threats
- Do not make false or misleading statements about the company, its products, or its competitors
- Refer all media inquiries to the Communications department

Employees who are authorized to manage official company social media accounts must follow the Social Media Guidelines published by the Marketing department. All official communications must be approved before publication.

### 2.6 Gifts and Entertainment

Acme Corporation recognizes that business relationships sometimes involve the exchange of modest gifts and entertainment. However, employees must exercise good judgment and ensure that such exchanges do not create actual or perceived conflicts of interest. The following guidelines apply:

| Category | Maximum Value | Approval Required |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|
| Business meals | $150 per person | No |
| Gifts received | $100 | Disclosure to manager |
| Gifts given | $100 | Manager approval |
| Event tickets | $250 | Director approval |
| Travel/lodging from vendor | Any value | VP approval |
| Cash or cash equivalents | Not permitted | N/A |

Gifts and entertainment involving government officials are subject to additional restrictions under applicable anti-corruption laws. Consult the Legal department before providing anything of value to a government official.

### 2.7 Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption

Acme Corporation is committed to conducting business ethically and in compliance with all applicable anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws, including the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act and the UK Bribery Act. Employees are strictly prohibited from:

- Offering, promising, or giving anything of value to a government official or any other person to obtain or retain business or secure an improper advantage
- Requesting or accepting bribes, kickbacks, or other improper payments
- Making facilitation payments to expedite routine government actions
- Using third parties, agents, or intermediaries to accomplish any prohibited act

Violations of this policy may result in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement. Employees who become aware of any potential violation must report it immediately through the company's Ethics Hotline or to the Legal department.

## 3. Remote Work Policy

### 3.1 Eligibility and Approval

Acme Corporation supports flexible work arrangements, including remote work, where business needs and job responsibilities permit. Remote work is not an entitlement and must be approved by the employee's manager and the Human Resources department.

Eligibility for remote work is based on the following criteria:

- The employee's role and responsibilities can be effectively performed remotely
- The employee has demonstrated consistent performance at or above expectations
- The employee has completed a minimum of 90 days of employment (probationary period)
- The employee has a suitable home workspace that meets ergonomic and safety requirements
- Reliable high-speed internet connectivity is available (minimum 50 Mbps download, 10 Mbps upload)
- The arrangement does not negatively impact team collaboration or customer service

### 3.2 Work Arrangements

Acme Corporation offers three types of work arrangements:

**Fully Remote:** The employee works from a location outside of an Acme Corporation office 100% of the time. Fully remote employees may be required to travel to a company office periodically for team meetings, training, or other business needs at company expense.

**Hybrid:** The employee splits their time between a company office and a remote location. The specific schedule (e.g., 3 days in-office, 2 days remote) is determined by the employee's manager based on team and business needs.

**In-Office:** The employee works from a company office during standard business hours. Occasional remote work may be permitted with manager approval for specific circumstances.

### 3.3 Remote Work Requirements

Remote employees must maintain a dedicated workspace that meets the following requirements:

- A private, quiet area suitable for professional work and video conferences
- An ergonomic workstation setup (desk, chair, monitor at appropriate height)
- Reliable high-speed internet connectivity with backup options
- Adequate lighting and ventilation
- Compliance with local fire safety and building codes
- Secure storage for company equipment and confidential documents

The company will provide the following equipment for approved remote workers:

- Laptop computer with standard software
- External monitor (one)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset for video conferencing
- VPN access and security software
- Monthly internet stipend of $75

### 3.4 Work Hours and Availability

Remote employees are expected to maintain regular work hours and be available during core business hours (10:00 AM to 3:00 PM in the employee's local time zone). Employees must be responsive to communications from colleagues, managers, and customers during these hours.

Specific expectations include:

- Responding to emails within 2 business hours during core hours
- Being available for scheduled meetings via video conference
- Updating calendar availability and status in the company's communication platform
- Notifying their manager promptly if they will be unavailable during core hours
- Logging work hours accurately in the company's time-tracking system

### 3.5 Communication and Collaboration

Effective communication is critical for remote work success. Remote employees are expected to:

- Participate actively in team meetings, stand-ups, and one-on-one sessions via video conference
- Use the company's approved communication tools (Slack, Microsoft Teams, Zoom) for all work-related communications
- Maintain their camera on during meetings unless there is a legitimate reason not to
- Provide regular status updates to their manager and team
- Document decisions, action items, and important information in shared workspaces
- Be proactive in reaching out to colleagues for collaboration and support

### 3.6 Security Requirements for Remote Workers

Remote employees must adhere to all IT security policies, with particular attention to the following:

- Use only company-provided or approved devices for work activities
- Connect to the company network exclusively through the approved VPN
- Enable full-disk encryption on all devices used for work
- Use multi-factor authentication for all company accounts
- Lock screens when stepping away from the workstation
- Ensure home Wi-Fi networks are secured with WPA3 encryption and strong passwords
- Do not use public Wi-Fi for work activities without VPN protection
- Store all work files on company-approved cloud storage (not local drives)
- Report any security incidents or suspicious activity immediately

### 3.7 Expense Reimbursement for Remote Workers

In addition to the monthly internet stipend, remote employees may be eligible for reimbursement of the following expenses:

| Expense Category | Maximum Reimbursement | Frequency |
|-----------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Home office setup | $1,500 | One-time |
| Ergonomic assessment | $300 | Annual |
| Office supplies | $200 | Annual |
| Internet service | $75 | Monthly |
| Co-working space | $250 | Monthly (with approval) |
| Phone plan upgrade | $50 | Monthly |

All expenses must be submitted through the expense management system with appropriate receipts and approved by the employee's manager within 30 days of incurring the expense.

## 4. Leave and Time-Off Policies

### 4.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)

Acme Corporation provides a comprehensive paid time off program that combines vacation, personal, and sick days into a single PTO bank. PTO accrues based on years of service as follows:

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Accrual | Maximum Balance |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 days (120 hours) | 180 hours |
| 3-5 years | 20 days (160 hours) | 240 hours |
| 6-10 years | 25 days (200 hours) | 300 hours |
| 11+ years | 30 days (240 hours) | 360 hours |

PTO accrues on a per-pay-period basis and is available for use in the pay period following accrual. Employees may carry over unused PTO to the following year up to the maximum balance. Any PTO in excess of the maximum balance at year-end will be forfeited. Upon separation from the company, accrued but unused PTO will be paid out in accordance with applicable law.

### 4.2 PTO Request and Approval Process

All PTO requests must be submitted through the company's HR management system at least:

- **1-3 days off:** 2 weeks advance notice
- **4-5 days off:** 4 weeks advance notice
- **More than 5 consecutive days:** 6 weeks advance notice
- **Unplanned absences (illness):** Notify manager before the start of the workday

Managers will review PTO requests considering business needs, team coverage, and fairness. While every effort will be made to accommodate employee preferences, approval is not guaranteed. During peak business periods (quarter-end, product launches), PTO requests may be limited to ensure adequate staffing.

### 4.3 Holidays

Acme Corporation observes the following paid holidays each year:

| Holiday | Date |
|---------|------|
| New Year's Day | January 1 |
| Martin Luther King Jr. Day | Third Monday in January |
| Presidents' Day | Third Monday in February |
| Memorial Day | Last Monday in May |
| Juneteenth | June 19 |
| Independence Day | July 4 |
| Labor Day | First Monday in September |
| Thanksgiving Day | Fourth Thursday in November |
| Day after Thanksgiving | Fourth Friday in November |
| Christmas Eve | December 24 |
| Christmas Day | December 25 |
| New Year's Eve | December 31 |

When a holiday falls on a Saturday, the preceding Friday will be observed. When a holiday falls on a Sunday, the following Monday will be observed. Employees required to work on a holiday will receive holiday premium pay at 1.5 times their regular rate.

### 4.4 Parental Leave

Acme Corporation provides generous parental leave to support employees who are welcoming a new child through birth, adoption, or foster placement.

**Birth Parents:** Up to 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave, beginning on the date of birth or as medically necessary prior to the expected due date. An additional 4 weeks of unpaid leave is available upon request.

**Non-Birth Parents, Adoptive Parents, and Foster Parents:** Up to 12 weeks of fully paid parental leave, beginning on the date of placement or birth.

Parental leave may be taken consecutively or intermittently within the first 12 months following the birth or placement of the child, subject to manager approval for intermittent schedules. Employees must provide at least 30 days' notice of their anticipated leave dates when possible.

During parental leave, all benefits will continue as if the employee were actively working. PTO will not accrue during unpaid portions of parental leave.

### 4.5 Bereavement Leave

Employees are entitled to paid bereavement leave following the death of a family member:

- **Immediate family** (spouse, child, parent, sibling): Up to 5 consecutive business days
- **Extended family** (grandparent, grandchild, in-law, aunt, uncle): Up to 3 consecutive business days
- **Close friend or colleague:** 1 business day

Additional unpaid leave may be granted at the manager's discretion. The company may request documentation (e.g., obituary, funeral program) to support bereavement leave requests.

### 4.6 Jury Duty and Civic Leave

Employees summoned for jury duty will receive their regular pay for up to 10 business days per calendar year. Employees serving jury duty must provide a copy of their summons to their manager and the HR department. If dismissed from jury service early in the day, employees are expected to return to work for the remainder of the day.

Employees are also entitled to up to 4 hours of paid time off to vote in federal, state, and local elections when polls are not open for at least 2 hours before or after the employee's scheduled work hours.

### 4.7 Military Leave

Acme Corporation complies with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA) and all applicable state laws regarding military leave. Employees who are members of the uniformed services will be granted leave for military duty and will be reemployed in accordance with applicable law. During the first 30 calendar days of military leave, employees will receive the difference between their military pay and their regular Acme Corporation salary, if the military pay is less.

### 4.8 Sabbatical Leave

Employees who have completed 7 or more years of continuous service are eligible for a paid sabbatical leave of 4 weeks (20 business days). Sabbatical leave is intended to provide employees with an opportunity for rest, personal enrichment, travel, or community service. Sabbatical eligibility renews every 7 years.

Sabbatical leave requests must be submitted at least 3 months in advance and approved by the employee's VP and the HR department. The timing of the sabbatical must be coordinated with business needs and team coverage.

### 4.9 Leave of Absence

Employees may request an unpaid leave of absence for personal reasons not covered by other leave policies. Unpaid leave of absence requests must be submitted in writing to the employee's manager and the HR department and are subject to approval based on business needs. During an unpaid leave of absence, benefits may continue at the employee's expense.

## 5. IT Security Policies

### 5.1 Acceptable Use Policy

All technology resources provided by Acme Corporation, including computers, networks, email, internet access, software, and cloud services, are company property and are intended for business use. Limited personal use is permitted, provided it does not interfere with work productivity, consume excessive resources, or violate any company policy or applicable law.

The following activities are strictly prohibited:

- Accessing, downloading, or distributing illegal, obscene, or offensive content
- Installing unauthorized software or applications on company devices
- Using company resources for personal commercial ventures or political activities
- Sharing login credentials or allowing unauthorized access to company systems
- Attempting to bypass security controls, firewalls, or access restrictions
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the company network
- Using company email or communication tools for mass solicitations or spam
- Downloading or using pirated software, media, or other copyrighted materials

### 5.2 Password and Authentication Policy

Strong authentication is the first line of defense against unauthorized access. All employees must adhere to the following password and authentication requirements:

**Password Requirements:**
- Minimum length of 14 characters
- Must include at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one digit, and one special character
- Cannot contain the user's name, username, or common dictionary words
- Cannot reuse any of the last 12 passwords
- Must be changed every 90 days for standard accounts and every 60 days for privileged accounts

**Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):**
- MFA is required for all company accounts, including email, VPN, cloud services, and internal applications
- Approved MFA methods include hardware security keys (preferred), authenticator apps, and push notifications
- SMS-based MFA is not permitted due to known security vulnerabilities
- Employees must register at least two MFA methods to ensure access in case one method is unavailable

**Account Lockout:**
- Accounts will be locked after 5 consecutive failed login attempts
- Locked accounts will be automatically unlocked after 30 minutes or can be unlocked by the IT Help Desk
- Accounts with 10 or more failed attempts within a 24-hour period will require IT Security review before unlocking

### 5.3 Device Security

All devices used to access company data and systems must meet the following security requirements:

**Company-Managed Devices:**
- Operating system and software must be kept up to date with the latest security patches
- Full-disk encryption must be enabled (BitLocker for Windows, FileVault for macOS)
- Endpoint detection and response (EDR) software must be installed and active
- Automatic screen lock after 5 minutes of inactivity
- USB storage devices are disabled by default; exceptions require IT Security approval

**Personal Devices (BYOD):**
- Personal devices may be used to access company email and approved cloud applications only
- The company's mobile device management (MDM) software must be installed
- Device must meet minimum OS version requirements (iOS 17+, Android 14+)
- Device must be protected with a PIN, password, or biometric authentication
- Remote wipe capability must be enabled
- Work data must be contained within the company's managed applications

### 5.4 Network Security

Acme Corporation maintains a multi-layered network security architecture to protect company data and systems. Employees must adhere to the following network security requirements:

- Always connect to the company network through the approved VPN when working remotely
- Do not connect to unsecured or public Wi-Fi networks without VPN protection
- Do not set up personal wireless access points, routers, or network devices in company offices
- Do not attempt to scan, probe, or test the company network without authorization
- Report any suspected network security incidents to the IT Security team immediately

The company monitors network traffic for security purposes and reserves the right to inspect, log, and analyze all network activity on company-owned networks and devices.

### 5.5 Email Security

Email is a primary attack vector for cybersecurity threats. Employees must exercise caution when handling email communications:

- Do not open attachments or click links from unknown or suspicious senders
- Verify the sender's identity before responding to emails requesting sensitive information
- Report phishing emails immediately by forwarding them to security@acmecorp.com
- Do not use personal email accounts for business communications
- Do not auto-forward company email to external email addresses
- Use email encryption when sending sensitive or confidential information externally
- Be cautious of emails with urgent or threatening language designed to provoke immediate action

### 5.6 Incident Response

All employees have a role in the company's incident response process. If you suspect a security incident, including a data breach, malware infection, unauthorized access, or physical security breach, take the following steps:

1. **Do not attempt to investigate or remediate the incident yourself**
2. **Report the incident immediately** to the IT Security team via email (security@acmecorp.com), phone (x5555), or the Security Incident Report form on the intranet
3. **Preserve evidence** by not deleting files, emails, or logs related to the incident
4. **Disconnect from the network** if you believe your device is actively compromised (unplug ethernet, disable Wi-Fi)
5. **Do not discuss the incident** with unauthorized persons, including on social media
6. **Cooperate fully** with the IT Security team's investigation

The IT Security team will classify the incident based on severity, coordinate the response, and communicate with affected parties as appropriate.

### 5.7 Software and Cloud Services

All software and cloud services used for company business must be approved by the IT department. This includes free, open-source, and trial software. The approval process ensures that software meets security, compliance, and compatibility requirements.

To request approval for new software or cloud services:

1. Submit a Software Request form through the IT Service Portal
2. Provide the business justification and the names of intended users
3. The IT Security team will conduct a security assessment
4. The Legal department will review licensing terms and data processing agreements
5. Upon approval, the IT department will procure and deploy the software

Employees must not use unauthorized cloud services (shadow IT) to store, process, or transmit company data. This includes personal accounts on services like Dropbox, Google Drive, or WeTransfer for business purposes.

## 6. Data Protection and Privacy

### 6.1 Data Classification

Acme Corporation classifies data into four categories to ensure appropriate handling and protection:

| Classification | Description | Examples | Handling Requirements |
|---------------|-------------|----------|----------------------|
| **Public** | Information intended for public disclosure | Marketing materials, press releases, job postings | No restrictions |
| **Internal** | Information for internal use only | Internal memos, organizational charts, meeting notes | Access limited to employees; no external sharing without approval |
| **Confidential** | Sensitive business information | Financial reports, customer data, contracts, strategic plans | Encrypted in transit and at rest; access on need-to-know basis; NDA required for external sharing |
| **Restricted** | Highly sensitive information | PII, PHI, payment card data, trade secrets, credentials | Encrypted at all times; strict access controls; logging and monitoring required; specific regulatory compliance required |

### 6.2 Data Handling Procedures

Employees must handle data in accordance with its classification level. The following procedures apply:

**Data Collection:**
- Collect only the minimum amount of data necessary for the stated business purpose
- Inform data subjects about the purpose of data collection and their rights
- Obtain appropriate consent where required by law
- Document the legal basis for data processing

**Data Storage:**
- Store data on company-approved systems and platforms only
- Ensure encryption is enabled for Confidential and Restricted data at rest
- Apply appropriate access controls based on data classification
- Do not store Confidential or Restricted data on personal devices or unauthorized cloud services

**Data Transmission:**
- Encrypt Confidential and Restricted data when transmitting over networks
- Use secure file transfer methods (SFTP, encrypted email) for external sharing
- Verify the recipient's identity and authorization before sharing sensitive data
- Do not transmit Restricted data via unencrypted email

**Data Retention:**
- Retain data only for as long as necessary for the stated business purpose or as required by law
- Follow the Data Retention Schedule published by the Legal department
- Securely dispose of data that has reached the end of its retention period

**Data Disposal:**
- Use approved data destruction methods (secure deletion, physical destruction)
- Obtain a certificate of destruction for physical media containing Restricted data
- Clear all data from devices before disposal, reassignment, or return

### 6.3 Privacy Principles

Acme Corporation is committed to protecting the privacy of employees, customers, partners, and other individuals whose personal information we process. Our privacy practices are guided by the following principles:

- **Lawfulness:** We process personal data only in accordance with applicable privacy laws and regulations
- **Purpose Limitation:** We collect and use personal data only for specified, legitimate business purposes
- **Data Minimization:** We collect only the personal data that is necessary for the stated purpose
- **Accuracy:** We take reasonable steps to ensure personal data is accurate and up to date
- **Storage Limitation:** We retain personal data only for as long as necessary
- **Integrity and Confidentiality:** We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data
- **Accountability:** We document our data processing activities and can demonstrate compliance

### 6.4 Data Subject Rights

Individuals whose personal data is processed by Acme Corporation have certain rights under applicable privacy laws, which may include:

- The right to access their personal data
- The right to correct inaccurate personal data
- The right to delete their personal data
- The right to restrict processing of their personal data
- The right to data portability
- The right to object to processing of their personal data
- The right to withdraw consent

Requests to exercise these rights should be directed to privacy@acmecorp.com. The Privacy team will respond to all valid requests within the timeframes required by applicable law (typically 30 days).

### 6.5 Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach involving personal information, Acme Corporation will:

1. Investigate the breach promptly and thoroughly
2. Contain the breach and take steps to prevent further unauthorized access
3. Assess the risk to affected individuals
4. Notify affected individuals and relevant supervisory authorities as required by applicable law
5. Document the breach and remediation actions taken
6. Implement measures to prevent similar breaches in the future

Employees who become aware of a potential data breach must report it immediately to the IT Security team and the Privacy team.

### 6.6 International Data Transfers

When personal data is transferred across international borders, Acme Corporation ensures that appropriate safeguards are in place to protect the data in accordance with applicable privacy laws. Approved transfer mechanisms include:

- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
- Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
- Adequacy decisions by relevant authorities
- Other legally approved mechanisms

Employees must consult the Legal department before initiating any new international data transfer.

## 7. Travel and Expense Policies

### 7.1 General Travel Guidelines

Business travel should be undertaken only when necessary and when remote alternatives (video conferencing, phone calls) are not adequate. All business travel must be approved in advance by the employee's manager and must comply with the company's travel and expense guidelines.

Employees should exercise good judgment and fiscal responsibility when incurring travel expenses. The company will reimburse reasonable and necessary business travel expenses that are properly documented and approved.

### 7.2 Travel Booking

All business travel must be booked through the company's designated travel management platform (TravelConnect). Booking through the platform ensures compliance with company policies, preferred vendor agreements, and duty of care requirements.

**Air Travel:**
- Economy class is standard for all domestic flights and international flights under 6 hours
- Premium economy is permitted for international flights of 6 hours or more
- Business class requires VP approval and is permitted for international flights of 8 hours or more
- First class is not permitted without CEO approval
- Flights should be booked at least 14 days in advance to secure the best rates
- Employees should select the lowest reasonable fare considering total trip cost and schedule

**Hotels:**
- Use company-preferred hotels when available
- Standard room accommodations within the following per-night limits:

| City Category | Maximum Nightly Rate |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Tier 1 (NYC, SF, London, Tokyo) | $300 |
| Tier 2 (Chicago, LA, Boston, Seattle) | $250 |
| Tier 3 (All other cities) | $200 |
| Tier 4 (International developing markets) | $175 |

- Extended stays (5+ nights) may qualify for reduced rates through the travel management platform

**Ground Transportation:**
- Use public transportation, airport shuttles, or ride-sharing services when practical
- Rental cars are permitted when public transportation is not practical or cost-effective
- Compact or mid-size vehicles are standard; larger vehicles require justification
- Personal vehicle mileage is reimbursed at the current IRS standard rate ($0.70/mile for 2026)

### 7.3 Meal Expenses

Reasonable meal expenses incurred during business travel will be reimbursed within the following daily limits:

| Meal | Domestic Limit | International Limit |
|------|---------------|---------------------|
| Breakfast | $25 | $35 |
| Lunch | $30 | $45 |
| Dinner | $50 | $75 |
| Daily Maximum | $105 | $155 |

Meal expenses for team dinners or client entertainment may exceed these limits with manager approval. Alcohol is limited to 2 drinks per meal and is not reimbursable during solo meals. Receipts are required for all meal expenses exceeding $25.

### 7.4 Expense Reporting

All business expenses must be submitted through the company's expense management system (ExpenseTrack) within 30 days of incurring the expense. Late submissions may result in delayed or denied reimbursement.

Expense reports must include:

- Date, location, and business purpose for each expense
- Itemized receipts for all expenses exceeding $25
- Attendee names for meals and entertainment expenses
- Currency conversion details for international expenses
- Manager approval before submission

The Finance department will review and process approved expense reports within 10 business days. Reimbursement will be deposited directly into the employee's payroll account.

### 7.5 Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are not reimbursable:

- Personal entertainment (movies, sightseeing, spa services)
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, and towing charges
- Personal phone calls (beyond reasonable limits)
- Laundry and dry cleaning (unless trip exceeds 5 days)
- Upgrades to airline seats, hotel rooms, or rental cars not pre-approved
- Travel insurance purchased independently (the company provides coverage)
- Expenses for companions or family members
- Minibar charges
- Lost or stolen personal property

### 7.6 Corporate Credit Card

Employees who travel frequently or incur regular business expenses may be issued a company corporate credit card. The corporate credit card is for business expenses only and must not be used for personal purchases.

Corporate credit card holders must:

- Reconcile transactions monthly in the expense management system
- Submit all receipts for corporate credit card charges
- Report lost or stolen cards immediately to the Finance department
- Return the card upon termination of employment or change of role
- Not allow any other person to use the card

Misuse of the corporate credit card may result in revocation of card privileges, disciplinary action, and personal liability for unauthorized charges.

## 8. Performance Review Process

### 8.1 Performance Management Philosophy

Acme Corporation believes that regular, constructive feedback is essential for employee development and organizational success. Our performance management process is designed to:

- Align individual goals with team and organizational objectives
- Provide employees with clear expectations and regular feedback
- Identify strengths and areas for development
- Support career growth and professional development
- Inform compensation decisions fairly and transparently

### 8.2 Performance Review Cycle

The performance review cycle at Acme Corporation consists of the following components:

**Goal Setting (January):**
- Employees and managers collaborate to set 3-5 SMART goals for the year
- Goals should be aligned with team objectives and the company's strategic priorities
- At least one goal should focus on professional development
- Goals are documented in the performance management system and reviewed quarterly

**Quarterly Check-ins (April, July, October):**
- Managers and employees meet for a 30-45 minute check-in each quarter
- Review progress toward annual goals and adjust as needed
- Discuss successes, challenges, and support needed
- Document key discussion points and action items

**Mid-Year Review (July):**
- A more comprehensive review of progress toward annual goals
- Assessment of competency development and behavioral expectations
- Adjustment of goals if business priorities have changed
- Written summary documented in the performance management system

**Annual Performance Review (December):**
- Comprehensive assessment of the employee's performance against goals and competencies
- Self-assessment by the employee
- Manager assessment with written narrative
- Calibration across teams to ensure consistency and fairness
- Performance rating assigned on the company's rating scale
- Development plan for the upcoming year

### 8.3 Performance Rating Scale

Acme Corporation uses a 5-point rating scale for annual performance reviews:

| Rating | Description | Definition |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; delivers extraordinary results; recognized as a top performer and role model |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Frequently exceeds expectations; delivers high-quality results above what is required |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Consistently meets all expectations; delivers solid, reliable results |
| 2 | Needs Improvement | Occasionally falls short of expectations; performance gaps require attention and a development plan |
| 1 | Unsatisfactory | Consistently fails to meet expectations; immediate improvement required; may result in performance improvement plan |

Rating distribution guidelines (not quotas) are used to ensure consistency:
- Ratings of 5: approximately 5-10% of employees
- Ratings of 4: approximately 20-30% of employees
- Ratings of 3: approximately 50-60% of employees
- Ratings of 2: approximately 5-10% of employees
- Ratings of 1: less than 5% of employees

### 8.4 Performance Improvement Plans

When an employee's performance is consistently below expectations, the manager may implement a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP). A PIP is a structured plan that outlines:

- Specific performance deficiencies and expected improvements
- Measurable goals and milestones with clear timelines
- Resources, training, and support available to the employee
- Frequency of check-in meetings (typically weekly)
- Consequences of failure to improve (up to and including termination)

PIPs typically span 30-90 days, depending on the nature of the performance issues. The employee, manager, and HR partner will meet at the beginning of the PIP period to discuss the plan and at regular intervals to review progress.

Successful completion of a PIP returns the employee to regular performance management. Failure to meet PIP objectives may result in further action, up to and including termination of employment.

### 8.5 Compensation and Performance

Annual compensation adjustments, including merit increases and bonus payouts, are directly tied to performance ratings. The following guidelines apply:

| Performance Rating | Merit Increase Range | Bonus Target Multiplier |
|-------------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| 5 - Exceptional | 6-10% | 1.5x - 2.0x |
| 4 - Exceeds | 4-6% | 1.1x - 1.5x |
| 3 - Meets | 2-4% | 0.9x - 1.1x |
| 2 - Needs Improvement | 0-2% | 0x - 0.5x |
| 1 - Unsatisfactory | 0% | 0x |

Actual increases and bonuses are subject to company financial performance, budget availability, and market competitiveness. Compensation decisions are reviewed and approved by the employee's VP and the HR department.

### 8.6 Promotions and Career Development

Acme Corporation is committed to developing employees and promoting from within whenever possible. Promotions are based on:

- Consistent performance at or above expectations
- Demonstration of competencies required for the next level
- Business need for the higher-level role
- Manager recommendation and leadership approval
- Availability of headcount and budget

Employees interested in career advancement should discuss their aspirations with their manager during check-in meetings and performance reviews. The company provides various development resources, including:

- Internal training programs and workshops
- External conference attendance and certification programs
- Tuition reimbursement (up to $5,250 per year for approved programs)
- Mentorship and coaching programs
- Cross-functional project assignments and rotational opportunities
- Leadership development programs for high-potential employees

## 9. Workplace Safety

### 9.1 Commitment to Safety

Acme Corporation is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for all employees, contractors, and visitors. Workplace safety is a shared responsibility, and every individual has a role in maintaining a safe workplace. The company complies with all applicable occupational safety and health regulations, including those set by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) and equivalent state agencies.

### 9.2 General Safety Rules

All employees must adhere to the following general safety rules:

- Report all workplace injuries, illnesses, and near-misses to your manager and the Safety team immediately, regardless of severity
- Report any unsafe conditions, equipment, or practices to your manager or the Safety team
- Follow all posted safety signs, warnings, and instructions
- Use personal protective equipment (PPE) as required for your job duties
- Keep work areas clean, organized, and free of tripping hazards
- Do not operate equipment or machinery unless you are trained and authorized
- Do not tamper with, disable, or bypass safety devices, guards, or equipment
- Know the location of emergency exits, fire extinguishers, first aid kits, and AED devices
- Participate in all required safety training and emergency drills

### 9.3 Emergency Procedures

Acme Corporation maintains emergency action plans for each facility. All employees must be familiar with the emergency procedures for their work location, including:

**Fire:**
1. Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station
2. Evacuate the building immediately using the nearest exit (do not use elevators)
3. Proceed to the designated assembly point for your floor or area
4. Report to your floor warden for a headcount
5. Call 911 from a safe location if the fire department has not been notified
6. Do not re-enter the building until the all-clear signal is given

**Medical Emergency:**
1. Call 911 or the company's emergency number (x9111) immediately
2. Provide first aid if you are trained and it is safe to do so
3. Use the AED if the person is unresponsive and not breathing
4. Do not move the person unless they are in immediate danger
5. Stay with the person until emergency responders arrive

**Severe Weather:**
1. Monitor weather alerts through the company's notification system
2. Move to the designated shelter area (interior rooms on the lowest floor)
3. Stay away from windows, doors, and outside walls
4. Remain in the shelter area until the all-clear signal is given

**Active Threat:**
1. Run: If you can safely exit the building, do so immediately
2. Hide: If you cannot exit, find a secure location, lock or barricade the door, turn off lights, silence your phone
3. Fight: As a last resort, be prepared to defend yourself using any available objects

### 9.4 Ergonomics

Acme Corporation recognizes that ergonomic hazards can lead to musculoskeletal disorders and other injuries. The company provides ergonomic assessments and equipment to help employees maintain a safe and comfortable workstation.

Employees should:

- Adjust chair height so that feet are flat on the floor and thighs are parallel to the floor
- Position the monitor at arm's length with the top of the screen at or slightly below eye level
- Keep the keyboard and mouse close to the body and at elbow height
- Take regular breaks (at least 5 minutes every hour) to stretch and move
- Use the sit-stand desk feature if available, alternating between sitting and standing throughout the day
- Report any discomfort or pain related to workstation setup to their manager and request an ergonomic assessment

### 9.5 Workplace Violence Prevention

Acme Corporation maintains a zero-tolerance policy for workplace violence. Workplace violence includes any act or threat of physical violence, harassment, intimidation, bullying, or other threatening or disruptive behavior that occurs at the work site or in connection with work activities.

Prohibited conduct includes:

- Physical assaults, threats of violence, or aggressive physical contact
- Verbal threats, intimidation, or abusive language
- Stalking, following, or surveilling a colleague
- Possession of weapons on company property (including parking lots)
- Damaging or destroying company property or the property of others
- Any conduct that a reasonable person would perceive as threatening

Employees who experience or witness workplace violence should report it immediately to their manager, the HR department, or the Security team. All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially. The company will take appropriate action to protect affected individuals and prevent recurrence, up to and including termination of the perpetrator and referral to law enforcement.

### 9.6 Substance Abuse Policy

Acme Corporation is committed to maintaining a drug-free and alcohol-free workplace. The following policies apply:

- The use, possession, distribution, or sale of illegal drugs on company property or during work hours is strictly prohibited
- Being under the influence of illegal drugs or alcohol during work hours or while operating company equipment is strictly prohibited
- The use of prescription medications that may impair job performance must be disclosed to the employee's manager and the HR department
- Alcohol may be served at company-sponsored events with management approval; employees are expected to consume responsibly
- The company reserves the right to conduct drug and alcohol testing in accordance with applicable law, including pre-employment, post-accident, reasonable suspicion, and random testing

Employees who are struggling with substance abuse are encouraged to seek help through the company's Employee Assistance Program (EAP), which provides confidential counseling and referral services at no cost.

## 10. Anti-Harassment and Equal Opportunity

### 10.1 Anti-Harassment Policy

Acme Corporation is committed to providing a workplace free from harassment of any kind. Harassment based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other protected characteristic is strictly prohibited.

Harassment includes, but is not limited to:

- Offensive jokes, slurs, epithets, or name-calling
- Physical assaults, threats, or intimidation
- Ridicule, mockery, or insults based on protected characteristics
- Displaying offensive objects, pictures, or symbols
- Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature
- Making employment decisions based on submission to or rejection of unwelcome conduct

### 10.2 Reporting Harassment

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it through any of the following channels:

- Their immediate manager or any other manager
- The Human Resources department
- The company's Ethics Hotline (anonymous reporting available)
- The Legal department

All reports will be investigated promptly, thoroughly, and as confidentially as possible. The company will not tolerate retaliation against any employee who reports harassment in good faith or participates in an investigation.

### 10.3 Investigation Process

Upon receiving a report of harassment, the HR department will:

1. Acknowledge receipt of the complaint within 24 hours
2. Assign a trained investigator to conduct the investigation
3. Interview the complainant, the respondent, and relevant witnesses
4. Review relevant documents, communications, and evidence
5. Make findings based on a preponderance of the evidence
6. Recommend appropriate corrective action
7. Communicate the outcome to the complainant and the respondent
8. Document the investigation and findings

Investigations will typically be completed within 30 days of receiving the complaint. Complex cases may require additional time, and the complainant will be kept informed of the status.

## 11. Disciplinary Procedures

### 11.1 Progressive Discipline

Acme Corporation generally follows a progressive discipline approach for addressing employee misconduct or policy violations. The steps of progressive discipline are:

1. **Verbal Warning:** A documented conversation between the manager and employee addressing the issue and expected improvement
2. **Written Warning:** A formal written notice describing the issue, previous discussions, expected improvement, and consequences of further violations
3. **Final Written Warning / Suspension:** A formal notice that further violations will result in termination; may include a suspension without pay of up to 5 days
4. **Termination:** Separation from employment

The company reserves the right to skip steps or proceed directly to termination for serious misconduct, including but not limited to theft, fraud, violence, harassment, substance abuse, insubordination, or gross negligence.

### 11.2 Appeals

Employees who believe that disciplinary action was taken unfairly or in error may appeal through the following process:

1. Submit a written appeal to the HR department within 10 business days of the disciplinary action
2. The HR department will review the appeal and may conduct additional investigation
3. A decision will be communicated to the employee within 15 business days
4. The appeal decision is final and binding
